Dadasaheb Phalke

Father of Indian Cinema

Here's a time line of Dadasaheb Phalke's life:

  • 1870 - Born in Maharashtra, India
  • 1885 - Joined the Sir J. J. School of Art, Bombay and completed a one-year course in drawing.
  • 1886 - Accompanied his elder brother, Shivrampant, to Baroda where he married a girl from Marathe family
  • 1890 - Joined Kala Bhavan, the Faculty of Fine Arts, at the Maharaja Sayajirao University of Baroda and completed a course in Oil painting and Watercolor painting.
  • 1892 - Was awarded a gold medal for creating a model of an ideal theatre at the Industrial Exhibition of Ahmedabad.
  • 1893 - Principal Gajjar of Kala Bhavan allowed Phalke to use the photo studio and laboratory of Kala Bhavan where he started his work under the name of "Shri Phalke's Engraving and Photo Printing"
  • 1895 - Decides to become a professional photographer and relocated to Godhra for doing business.
  • 1900 - His business did not do well in Godhra and he lost his wife and a child during the plague epidemic in the city.
  • 1901 - At the end of this year, Phalke began to hold the public performances of magic using professional name of Professor Kelpha with letters of his last name in reverse order
  • 1902 - was married to Girija Karandikar, niece of proprietor of Kirloskar Natak Mandali. Girija was renamed as Saraswati after the marriage.
  • 1903 - got a job as a photographer and draftsman at the Archaeological Survey of India.
  • 1906 - Was not satisfied with his job, and then resigned his job and set up a printing press at Lonavla under the name of "Phalke Engraving and Printing Works" with R. G. Bhandarkar as a partner
  • 1908 - Purushottam Mavji was replaced for Bhandarkar as a partner and the press was renamed as "Laxmi Art Printing Works".
  • 1911 - Phalke with his elder son Bhalchandra went to see a film, Amazing Animals, at the America India Picture Palace.
  • 1913 - Released his film "Raja Harischandra" which was considered to be first full-length Indian feature film.
  • 1944 - 16 febraury the day he Died in Nashik, Maharasthra, India.
